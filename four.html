NIM Game Variation (Prime NIM by Claude E. Shannon)

Prime NIM is played the same way that NIM is except players must diminish heaps only by prime numbers, including 1 as a prime. As stated by Shannon himself, The game is actually a bit of mathematical hoax since at first sight it seems to involve deep additive properties of prime numbers. Actually the only property involved is that all multiples of the first non-prime are non-primes.

The first nonprime is 4. The strategy therefore is merely to regard each heap as being equal to the remainder when its number is divided by 4 and then to play standard NIM strategy with these modulo-4 numbers. If 1 is not counted as being prime, the strategy is less simple in standard play and is so complicated in misere play that it is believes that it has ever been solved.
